I lead a team of full-stack engineers in eBay's global shipping division, with team members based in London, Toronto, and Cyprus. Our primary focus is enhancing the revenue-sensitive post-sales shipping experience for sellers on the web in the US, UK, German, Canadian, and Australian markets.

We handle over 25 million label purchases per month, supporting a variety of payment methods and workflows including: pay up front, pay at store, bulk shipping, returns, tracking, insurance/claims, [cross-border exports](https://www.ebay.co.uk/sellercentre/global-sales/how-to-export){:target="_blank"}{:title="Cross-Border Exports on eBay"}, and ship-to [eBay Vault](https://www.ebay.com/vault){:target="_blank"}{:title="eBay Vault"} or [eBay Authentication centres](https://www.ebay.co.uk/help/selling/selling-tools/ebay-authenticity-guarantee?id=4644){:target="_blank"}{:title="eBay Authentication Guarantee centres"}. We build Single Page Applications in [React](https://reactjs.org/){:target="_blank"}{:title="React"}, complemented by back-end microservices in [Node.js](https://nodejs.org/){:target="_blank"}{:title="Node.js"}.

My responsibilities include: fostering good engineering, project prioritisation, capacity planning, stakeholder engagement, career development, performance calibration, and recruitment.

I empower engineers to be customer-focused and consider long-term platform architecture and maintainability over short-term results, resulting in a significant decrease in production issues and customer-reported defects.
