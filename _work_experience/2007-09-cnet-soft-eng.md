In 2007, CNET Networks had separate localised versions of [CNET](https://www.cnet.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="CNET"} and [ZDNet](https://www.zdnet.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="ZDNet"} for the UK, AU, DE, FR and SG/Asia markets. I focused primarily on UK web properties and collaborated with engineers from international teams to develop shared solutions to common challenges. Eventually, we established a central team creating a framework that provided routing, caching, and CDN integration out-of-the-box. I delivered training for the framework in the Paris office to FR and DE engineers, and presented our achievements at our off‑site in Singapore. I also assisted US teams with bug-fixing and feature development on [GameSpot](https://www.gamespot.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="GameSpot"}, [TV.com](https://www.tv.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="TV.com"} and [TechRepublic](https://www.techRepublic.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="TechRepublic"}.

In 2008, the broadcasting company [CBS](https://www.cbs.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="CBS"} acquired CNET Networks, as a way to revitalise their online presence.
