In 2020, Red Ventures acquired some of [ViacomCBS](https://www.viacomcbs.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="ViacomCBS"}'s non-broadcast brands, and I moved with that acquisition. I reported to the new VP of Engineering, and my main task was overseeing the migration of online video systems and workflows from what was previously established under ViacomCBS. This included undertaking vendor evaluations with the product team, planning transition targets for the Transition Services Agreement (TSA), staying on top of engineering deliverables, and managing stakeholder expectations across both organisations.

I architected and was a lead engineer for the replacement video‑on‑demand (VOD) solution. It was designed to be able to serve up to a billion video play sessions per year to handle the brands that had moved to Red Ventures. We used services provided by [GCP](https://cloud.google.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="GCP"}, [Bitmovin](https://bitmovin.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="Bitmovin"} and [Fastly](https://www.fastly.com/){:target="_blank"}{:title="Fastly"}. As part of the transition, we were able to remove a content management vendor, saving US$430k in the first year, and US$275k pa after that.
